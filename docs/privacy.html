🦊 Rockimals — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

The short version: Rockimals collects no personal data at all. No accounts, no ads, no analytics, no tracking. Ever.

Who we are

Rockimals is a kid-friendly space app made by Ibrahim Uylas. It turns NASA's public near-Earth asteroid data into a playful "space zoo" for children. You can contact us at info@duniaops.com.

What we collect

Nothing. Rockimals has:

  • No accounts or sign-in — we never ask for a name, email, or any other personal information.
  • No advertising of any kind.
  • No analytics or tracking — we do not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
  • No messages, photos, location, or contacts — the app never asks for these permissions.

What stays on the device

Game progress — points, badges, followed animals, best scores, and settings — is stored only on the device itself. It never leaves the device, and deleting the app deletes it.

The one internet connection the app makes

Rockimals downloads its asteroid information from NASA's public NeoWs API (api.nasa.gov), a free public science service. This is an ordinary, anonymous web request: it contains no personal information about the user and no identifiers from the app. Like any web request, NASA's servers technically receive the device's IP address to deliver the response; Rockimals adds nothing to it. If the device is offline, the app uses bundled sample data instead and makes no connection at all.

External links and the parent gate

The only links out of the app (to NASA/JPL science pages) live inside the "grown-up facts" section, behind a parental gate designed so that young children cannot follow them on their own.

Children's privacy

Rockimals is designed for children and is built to comply with children's privacy laws, including COPPA (US) and GDPR-K (EU). Because the app collects no personal data from anyone, it collects no personal data from children.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever changes in a way that affects privacy, we will update this page and the "last updated" date above before the change ships.
